What is the importance of recognizing facial disfigurement as a disability?

by Michael Wu

"My entire school life I had the cleft defect and so was the discrimination. My classmates used to beat me, abuse me verbally & physically, just because I did not look like them. I had a defect and the people around me was not able to accept me for who I was." -Raj

Currently, facial disfigurement is not treated of equal importance to other disabilities, such as autism.

This website is dedicated to informing the public and highlighting the importance of recognizing facial disfigurement as a disability. Proper recognition of this disability is the only way to create positive changes for people dealing with this issue. If society chooses to ignore this major problem, then the people that are dealing with it will stay invisible.

The website will also contain information on the different aspects of facial disfigurement. There will be statistics, as well as personal anecdotes from people that have this disability. This includes people born with the disability, or people that had it later on in life due to other causes.

To learn more on how you can help, go to www.myFace.org/.
